Myths about coffee

Coffee is among the most popular beverages in the world, second only to water. Over time, many myths have developed about the benefits of coffee.

Let's see which ones are true.
1. Coffee is a very good energizer if you drink it before a physical effort or at the beginning of a hard day - TRUE. But be careful! It is not recommended to drink coffee on an empty stomach.
2. Coffee is not fattening, but keeps the body in balance - TRUE. This is true if it is black, without sugar or milk.
3. Coffee is a good concentration aid and improves short-term memory - TRUE.
4. Coffee is a good antioxidant - TRUE. According to an American study, coffee provides the body with more antioxidants than fruits and vegetables.
5. It helps digestion and you can lose weight - TRUE. Coffee helps burn fat and boosts your metabolic rate.
6. Helps fight migraines - TRUE. The effect of caffeine on these unpleasant conditions is scientifically proven. A little trick: pour lemon juice into your coffee for maximum results.
7. Gets rid of cellulite - TRUE. Most anti-cellulite creams contain caffeine.
8. Coffee dehydrates - MIT. Coffee is a daily source of hydration when consumed in moderate amounts.
9. Coffee is not recommended during pregnancy - MIT. Studies have shown that moderate coffee consumption has no harmful effect on the unborn child. However, take care to eat properly!
10. Coffee is good after alcohol consumption - MIT. After drinking a large amount of alcohol, coffee is contraindicated because it makes you feel worse.

So how do you drink your coffee?
